# Submission Positioning for LA Hacks and ZETIC

PhysioPal should be positioned first as a **healthcare recovery app** and second as a **privacy-first on-device AI product**.[cite:2][cite:75]

## Best track fit

For LA Hacks, the strongest primary framing is healthcare because the product addresses the supervision gap in physical therapy and supports safer at-home recovery.[cite:2]

## Best ZETIC framing

The strongest sponsor framing is:

**PhysioPal uses Melange to run exercise supervision directly on the iPhone, delivering real-time form correction with private on-device inference and optional cloud escalation only when human help is needed.**[cite:75][cite:2]

## What to emphasize in the demo

- The camera feed does not leave the device during supervision.[cite:75][cite:60]
- The app’s core value still works without a constant cloud dependency.[cite:75]
- The cloud is only used for secondary escalation actions like calling or joining a PT session.[cite:75]
- The user experience is practical, supportive, and usable in a real healthcare-adjacent context.[cite:75]

## What judges will care about most

### For ZETIC

- Core functionality is truly on-device.[cite:75]
- Melange is actually used in the app, not just mentioned.[cite:75]
- The app feels useful and real, not like a thin demo wrapper.[cite:75]
- Latency and privacy are explained clearly.[cite:75]

### For LA Hacks generally

- The product solves a clear human problem.[cite:2]
- The prototype is end-to-end and demoable.[cite:2]
- The UI is polished and understandable.[cite:2][cite:75]

## Suggested one-line pitch

**PhysioPal is a digital physiotherapist that uses ZETIC Melange for private, real-time on-device exercise supervision, helping patients recover safely at home and escalating to a human therapist only when needed.**[cite:75][cite:2]

## Suggested “on-device vs cloud” slide

Use a simple two-column explanation in the pitch deck or demo:

| On-device | Cloud / secondary |
|---|---|
| Pose estimation and form correction.[cite:75][cite:57] | PT call initiation.[cite:75][cite:53] |
| Rep counting and exercise evaluation.[cite:75] | Optional video consultation join flow.[cite:75] |
| Health-based routine adjustment.[cite:75] | Optional reporting / follow-up sharing.[cite:75] |

This explicit separation directly maps to what the ZETIC challenge asks teams to demonstrate.[cite:75]
